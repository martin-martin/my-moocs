Chronobiology

How do rhythms affect our lives. Every cell has a tiny watch inside, and we use external cues (especially light) to synchronize them all together. We evolved with clear and reliable Zeitgeber, using the light/dark hours, the seasonal temperature changes, the moon phases and some other animals also the tides, to orientate and synchronize a collection of hopelessly individual cells that need to work together to keep such a complex system going. Modern use of artificial lighting and central heating brings lots of confusion within.
