Certificates I collected while learning Online

I took my first peek into MOOCs in the 2012 AI Class, however I did not finish it then. The concepts of studying online were appealing to me, but maybe the content was a little bit too difficult for me at that time. I did, however, spend a worthwile and interesting time while studying for the class in weekly meet-ups with friends of mine. And in doing so, I got myself exposed to some concepts of CS, maths and statistics, that I would encounter more in the coming years.

On this page here I will try to collect all the certificates I have gained since getting into MOOCs big time, which was towards the end of 2014. This aims to be a chronological collection, with the newest ones further up. On top, however, I'll put the Specializations I've finished and also the ones I'm currently working on.

Specializations

Introduction to Programming Nanodegree

Learned to use HTML, CSS, some JavaScript and practiced Python programming. Among many other things, it set the foundation for me making this website :)

Data Analyst Nanodegree

Still working on it, here's a link to my projects on github. So far I've encountered the SciPy stack (using iPython notebooks, numpy, pandas, matplotlib) and currently starting to work with cleaning and querying OSM data.

2015

Using Python to Access Web Data

Took it as a light introduction to data acquistion that I'm learning more in-depth in the Data Analyst ND.

Process Mining: Data Science in Action

Bringing data to life by mining the processes within. Creating process maps, working with ProM and Disco.

6.00.1x: Introduction to Computer Science and Programming Using Python

A quite different Introduction to Python programming, that felt like a great additions to my knowledge until then. It allowed me to approach the topic of programming much more theoretically, and I'd say it encompasses quite some aspects of Computer Science.

Principles of Computing (Part 1)

It's a successor to IIPWP, however I did not enjoy the course so much. Stopped working on it at some point.

Chicken Behaviour and Welfare

A beautiful course that taught me a lot of respect for chickens. Their peaks are extremely sensitve and great perceptive organs! I'd recommend the course to anyone.

Introduction to Mathematical Thinking

It's one of my big tasks to train my math brain. This course was pleasant and interesting. I always liked logic, and I agree that one should take a walk/climb/bikeride as a break when brooding over some understanding.

Circadian Clocks: how rhythms structure life

The processes behind and effects of natural rhythms in our lifes (and how they are getting damaged daily). Every cell has its own clock, and we use Zeitgeber (especially light!) to synchronize. Records of births in Spain showed a annual oscillation until: the advent of electrical light.

Welcome to Game Theory

This course made my mind go 'whoa!' pretty often. Concepts of Game Theory jumped into all aspects of my life, while I watched. There's a stable balance that situations converge to - but there are more stable situations. And only because one is happening, doesn't mean it's the best! (I guess usually it isn't) But because every move from there makes things worse, it's difficult to look for and find better.

Origins - Formation of the Universe, Solar System, Earth and Life

Life, the Universe and Everything. The course was very well made and I learned a lot about physics, astronomy (especially those metorites stayed with me), geology and paleobiology. Very thorough and insightful!

Gamification

Sounded interesting in terms of concept, but the course wasn't very. Got still quite far, but didn't complete it.

An Introduction to Consumer Neuroscience & Neuromarketing

This course had a very interesting topic, and the lectures were pleasant. However, the written projects I got to peer review showed that understanding didn't come too easily to everyone.

Dino 101 : Dinosaur Paleobiology

I'm a Dino-kid. Knew the names, had the magazines and loads of hard-plastic figures. This course was a dive into my and earth's past. Learned a lot of new things, and re-learned some others. When I was small, dinosaurs didn't have feathers yet ;)

2014

There's still much more to learn, but I got some insights into the inner functioning of the internet. Listened to inventors talk, thought about the layer structure of TCP/IP and managed to grasp some basics.

Epidemics - the Dynamics of Infectious Diseases

Extremely interesting and well done! I did the whole course and learned a lot - however no certificate because I didn't post in the Forums :/ So here's my custom made one.

Programming for Everybody (Python)

Yet another Intro to Python, a different and much lighter approach. The book is great (I read just a bit), and the course was a great way to relax a bit while still being on topic. Enjoyable!

Process Mining: Data Science in Action

Taught me a lot about processes and was a lively way to learn about Data Science. I enjoyed this course so much, that I re-took it in 2015, even paying for a Verified certificate

How to Learn Maths: For Students

Totally sweet! Made me start playing with numbers, and visualize operations with coloured squares. I'm still planning to finish this one, but keeping it around for motivational boosts :)

Your Body in the World: Adapting to Your Next Big Adventure

A light and sweet course about Human Physiology. A guy tries getting cold, warm, high up, etc.

Introductory Human Physiology

Gained some insights into how it all works inside of myself. Good course, covering cell biology up to organ functions. Worked on it quite some, but didn't finish. While swimming lengths in the pool, I was thinking about the molecular processes happening to me!

An Introduction to Interactive Programming in Python

My first MOOC learning Python. I coded some little games. Especially enjoyed the very well done Peer Review System that allowed me to see a lot of other people's code and learn from giving feedback.

Personal & Family Financial Planning

Worked through a quarter of the course. Got some practice and thinking about personal finances.

From the Big Bang to Dark Energy

I have a weakness for Astronomy. I find it thoroughly exciting to understand for a short moment how tiny we are as compared to the distances and sizes that roam the universe. I'm just a tiny dot on a dot that is nothing but a tiny dot in another (slightly bigger) tiny dot revolving around a dot that is still pretty damn small and represents little areas in a growing space with many (but compared to the size only a few) small clusters of all these same dots I talked about before.

Child Nutrition and Cooking

Thinking about US cooking- and eating-habits, and learning a little about government tries to decrease obesity. Did it in night shifts.

Managing Your Time, Money, and Career: MBA Insights for Undergraduates

Great tips and some handling of calculations. Got some insights into US culture, too. And yes: Compounding interest!

Economics 1: Principles of Economics

Whoa! This was really good. My first real introduction to Economics ever, and so well delivered and gripping! I especially enjoyed the Orange-selling-game.
